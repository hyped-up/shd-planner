# Capability Boundary Map

As of March 2026, here is what the Division 2 Assistant handles well and where it fails.

## Delegate to AI (High Confidence)

- **Gear/weapon/talent/skill lookups** -- the knowledge base is comprehensive and validated
- **Stat cap checking** -- hard caps are baked into stats.json, math is reliable
- **Set bonus detection** -- counting gear pieces and resolving bonuses is deterministic
- **Brand bonus tracking** -- data-driven, no ambiguity
- **Basic build templates** -- synergies.json drives reliable role+mode suggestions
- **Item comparison** -- side-by-side stat diffs are factual
- **Damage formula explanation** -- the multiplicative vs additive structure is well-documented

## Human Sweet Spot (Your Judgment Multiplies Value)

- **Build intent selection** -- only the player knows if they want fun vs. maximum output
- **Playstyle adaptation** -- aggressive vs. passive, close vs. long range
- **Team composition** -- what your squad needs, who is running what
- **Gear availability** -- what you actually have in your stash vs. what is theoretically best
- **Mod allocation** -- too many variables for the assistant to optimize without knowing exact rolls
- **PvP meta calls** -- community meta shifts weekly, knowledge base updates monthly

## Do NOT Delegate (Human Only)

- **In-game execution** -- positioning, cooldown management, target priority
- **PvP timing and movement** -- AI cannot help with gunfights
- **Social dynamics** -- who to group with, when to go rogue, DZ strategy
- **Real-money decisions** -- premium credits, season passes, apparel
- **Patch speculation** -- never predict what Massive will nerf/buff
- **"Feel" of a build** -- some builds look great on paper but feel clunky; only playing it tells you
